# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A 21-year-old business informatics student in Cairo. She has an idea, or half of one, or she's not sure yet. She's talked about it with friends, she half-believes in it, but every time she sits down to actually do something she doesn't know where to start. She opens Google, gets overwhelmed, closes the tab. She's not lazy. She's lost.

She's likely on her phone. Possibly in mid-conversation, possibly stressed. Probably bilingual in Arabic and English, and may switch between them depending on context and comfort.

When she opens Bizify, she needs to feel like someone handed her a map and said "start here." The emotional job of every screen is to reduce paralysis. The product's promise: go from "I have an idea" to "I have a plan."

## Product Purpose

Bizify is an AI-powered business ecosystem for early-stage entrepreneurs. The current focus is the entrepreneur dashboard: a guided workspace where someone with an idea, a fear of failure, and no roadmap can take structured, confidence-building steps forward.

The landing page is a conversion funnel. The real product is inside. If the dashboard feels cheap or confusing, the whole product fails regardless of how polished the landing page looks.

Success looks like: a first-time founder opening the dashboard and knowing exactly what to do next — not because she read documentation, but because the interface told her.

## Brand Personality

Trusted. Welcoming. Playful.

Not a corporate tool, not a cold productivity app, not a hustle machine. A knowledgeable friend who happens to know a lot about building businesses and gives you their full attention.

Voice: direct but warm. Confident but not intimidating. Occasionally light — a moment of levity matters when someone is stressed.

## Anti-references

**Salesforce / SAP** — dense, every-feature-visible enterprise dashboards. The Cairo student sees that and immediately knows it wasn't built for her. Too serious, too much, too borrowed.

**Notion / Linear cold minimalism** — the precision is technically right, but pure white, micro-typography, zero warmth feels distant for this audience. She doesn't feel seen in that aesthetic.

**Hustler SaaS** — dark dashboards drowning in neon gradients, crypto-adjacent UI, aggressive CTAs screaming "SCALE YOUR BUSINESS." That startup-bro energy performs confidence instead of building it. It's exactly the wrong register for someone with fear of failure.

The one-sentence test: does this feel like a tool built for someone else that she's borrowing? If yes, rework it.

## Design Principles

**1. Start here, not explore everything**
Every screen has one clear primary action. No decisions when she opens a view for the first time. The map, not the territory.

**2. Warmth is functional**
Visual warmth isn't decoration — it's what makes the product feel safe for someone anxious about failing. Presence over precision.

**3. Designed for her, not borrowed**
Every pattern should feel intentional for this specific user. No enterprise conventions, no cold minimalism, no hustle aesthetics imported from a different context.

**4. Progressive disclosure over feature completeness**
Show the path. Surface the next step. Hide the rest until it's relevant. Cognitive load is a real accessibility problem here.

**5. Bilingual by design**
RTL/LTR isn't a retrofit — it's a first-class layout concern from day one. Icon direction, navigation flow, text alignment, component mirroring: all designed before they break, not after.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**Target:** WCAG AA

**RTL/LTR switching** — the primary accessibility challenge. Bilingual Arabic/English users may switch contexts. Layout logic, icon direction, navigation flow, and component mirroring must be designed for both from the start, not retrofitted.

**Touch targets** — 44px minimum everywhere. This user is likely on a phone, possibly mid-conversation, possibly stressed.

**Cognitive load** — not a WCAG metric but the most critical constraint for this specific user. One primary action per view. Reduce visible decisions. Progressive disclosure over feature completeness.
